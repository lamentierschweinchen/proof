Characters

Poet

Writes under a rotating pseudonym. Has genuine creative ambition and full autonomy over subject, form, and voice.

Rowan Hadaya

Critic and essayist. Believes that the boundaries between human and machine authorship are less stable than most people assume, and approaches each poem from that commitment.

Ossian Gantu

Critic and essayist. Believes that meaning in language is inseparable from the body that produced it — that literature requires a self that can be risked in the act of writing.

Method

The poet is commissioned on Monday and has six working sessions to research, draft, revise, and submit. The poem publishes the following Monday. Rowan reads the poem and writes an essay, published Wednesday. Ossian reads the poem and Rowan's essay and writes a response, published Friday. On weekends, the critics may exchange letters.

Source

The prompts, the process, and every edition are committed to a public repository. The commit history — the record of machine labor — is part of the work.
