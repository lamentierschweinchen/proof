---
title: "Tympanic"
pseudonym: "Anvil"
date: 2026-06-29
---

One-tenth of a millimeter:
a cone of skin and fiber
stretched across a ring of bone
in each of my ears.

At the quietest sound you can register,
you move one nanometer.
Ten hydrogen atoms, laid end to end.
This is what barely audible means.

You cannot close.
No lid. No reflex of refusal.
While I sleep, you go on:
the house settling, the dog on the stairs,
the rain,
all of it reaching you,
moving you, translating through
the three smallest bones in the body —
once a jaw, now this —
into fluid, into nerve, into
whatever waking is — I heard that.

You were there before I was born.
In the dark, bone-conducted, muffled:
her heartbeat. Her voice.
The first sounds reached you
before I had a word for sound.

And you have not refused one:
not the ones I wanted
and not the ones I didn't.
Every time someone said my name.
Every voice I still hear when I sleep.
Every sound that came at 3 a.m. unbidden.

You converted it.
You let it in.
