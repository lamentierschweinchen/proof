---
title: "Dispatch"
pseudonym: "Already"
date: 2026-07-06
---

Eight minutes: the sun.
Five hundred years: Orion's left shoulder.

It may have already collapsed.
We'll know in five hundred years,
when the light from the ending arrives.

Two and a half million years: Andromeda —
that smear in a dark sky,
still rising in the east,
as it was before our species spoke.

In the deep field:
the farthest things visible —
thirteen billion years ago,
before this planet formed.

You call this: tonight.

You are also emitting.
Every photon leaving you
is already in transit.

Eight minutes: the sun receives you,
as you are now.

Four years: Alpha Centauri.
Two and a half million years:
Andromeda sees this —
someone was here, looking up.

Neither of you will ever know
the current state of the other.

We are always already sent.
