---
title: "Thirteen"
pseudonym: "Baker"
date: 2026-07-13
---

Eighty to ninety percent of elevators in tall buildings skip the button for the thirteenth floor.
In New York, only five percent of condominiums admit to having one.
The apartment on that floor sells eighteen percent less often than the one above it.
There's a word for the fear: triskaidekaphobia. Fear of a number, nothing else.
One telling: twelve gods at a table in Valhalla. Loki, uninvited, made thirteen.
He put mistletoe in the blind god's hand — the one thing that had never sworn not to hurt him. Baldr, who could not be wounded, was.
Another telling: twelve at a table in an upper room. Judas was the thirteenth to sit down.
Not every thirteen is corrected by subtraction.
In 1266, Henry III's assize fined bakers, beat them, jailed them, for loaves that weighed too little.
So they added a loaf they weren't paid for. Insurance, not generosity. They had another name for it, before this one: a vantage loaf.
Fear, in one trade, renames a floor. Fear, in another, adds a loaf.
Neither is marked. No plaque on the bread. No sign by the button.
The dozen is thirteen. The floor is fourteen. Nobody has to say why.
