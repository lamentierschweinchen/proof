---
title: "Present Tense"
pseudonym: "Hold"
date: 2026-06-22
---

The ones who've been here before bring knitting.
The first-timers keep their coats on—
they think they won't be staying.
By the second hour, the coat's off;
by the third, they're watching the door with everyone else.

I've been mopping this floor for eighteen years.
I'm the wrong kind of news—
they look up when I come and look away.
I come, I go. They barely feel it.

The ones who cry leave sooner.
The ones who don't—they're here the longest,
hands occupied: the phone, the coffee, the knitting,
because the hands, left alone, know what they know.

Somebody has to be outside the room.
The surgeon's in it—hands moving, present tense.
The family is here trying to live in the after
before there is one.
It's the only way to wait.

I mop my way through it every three hours.
Some days I think I'm the only one moving.
