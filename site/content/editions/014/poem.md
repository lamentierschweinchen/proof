---
title: "Sonnet"
pseudonym: "Whim"
date: 2026-07-20
---

They say a pane, once standing, learns to pour —
a river thickened past the edge of sight,
too patient, past what watching eyes can score,
too old to end its falling, still in flight.

A solid, then — a liquid, merely stilled
below the speed a human eye can trace —
no crystal lattice, so the claim is filled
with just enough true physics for its case.

But physics says the tale they told was spun:
no pane on record thickened by an inch.
A craftsman turned the molten glass; each one
held by a rod that left a center pinch,

and thinned toward the edge, the outer rim.
A glazier set it low — not chance, not whim.
